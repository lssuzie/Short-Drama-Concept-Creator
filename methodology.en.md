# Short Drama Creative Methodology  

> A structured creative methodology distilled from observations of hit short dramas in the H2 2025Q1 2026 market.

## 设计理念 (Design Philosophy)

This methodology is not an instruction set for "having AI write scripts." It is a **creative stimulation methodology**.

Good ideas do not emerge from thin air, nor are they simple copies of market hits. Good ideas arise at the intersection of **market** and **the creator's personal traits**.

**Core Insight:** Existing Agent harness tools excel at deterministic tasks like code execution, but perform poorly on creative work requiring flexibility. Direct generation with large models is flexible but fails to capture accumulated experience and methodology. This methodology positions itself between the two  it uses a structured approach to capture creator experience and judgment criteria while preserving the creative flexibility of large language models.

## 核心规则 (Core Rules)

### 规则零：从幻想开始，不从问题开始 (Rule 0: Start from Fantasy, Not from Problems)

Short dramas are adult fairy tales. The audience isn't here to watch their own suffering  they're here to see their suffering resolved.

Don't start with "what pain points do viewers have?" Start with "what kind of person do viewers want to become?"

**Three Sources of Fantasy ():**

1. **Ability Fantasy ()**  She's incredible, dominating everyone in a specific domain
   - Example: crushing ancient aristocrats with modern business thinking
   - Example: using regional personality to "reshape" rich-family rules

2. **Identity Fantasy ()**  She has secrets  looks ordinary but hides an astonishing other side
   - Example: 18-year-old appearance with an elder's soul
   - Example: pretending to be family but becoming real family

3. **Action Fantasy ()**  She acts first  does what everyone wants to do but dares not
   - Example: marrying first then retaliating on the spot
   - Example: best friends transmigrate together, breaking the fourth wall

**Tone Check ():**
- ✅ "Wow she's amazing, I want to be like that"  Correct
-  "Sigh she's so tragic, but good thing she made it through"  Wrong, change it
-  "She's so strong, I should work hard too"  Wrong, this is chicken soup, not a short drama

### 喜剧公式 (Comedy Formula)

Data from H2 2025Q1 2026 hits: short dramas with comedic elements break out more easily.

Comedy naturally carries: surprise, contrast, relaxation, satisfaction, status gap, situational displacement.

**Formula: Fantasy Trigger  Extreme Identity Misplacement  Family/Emotional Relations  Comedy  Healing**

| Element | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Fantasy Trigger (Fantasy Trigger) | An absurd "trigger" that fires the story | transmigration, rejuvenation, mind-reading, rebirth |
| Extreme Identity Misplacement (Extreme Identity Misplacement) | Greater identity gap created by trigger is better | 18yo appearance  elder's soul; best friends dual-transmigration |
| Family/Emotional Relations (Family/Emotional Relations) | Real emotional pain points beneath absurd shell | mother-daughter, mother-in-law/daughter-in-law, husband-wife, best-friend |
| Comedy () | Humor from identity clash must appear every episode | daily collisions from identity contrast |
| Healing (Healing) | After laughter, audience feels warmth | emotional reconciliation beneath absurd shell |

**Validation Method:** Describe your concept in one sentence and check if it contains at least 3 of the 5 elements.

### Strong Heroine Principle (Strong Heroine Principle)

- The audience follows the **heroine**, not the CP
- Every turning point is her decision
- He falls for her because she's too strong, not because of fate
- CP is seasoning, not the main course

---

### 规则一：主引擎是"看她怎么做" (Rule 1: "Watch What She Does" as Main Engine)

What viewers chase isn't "will they get together?"  it's "what will she do next?"

- ✅ She crushes ancient aristocrats with modern business thinking  "what she does" is the engine
-  She meets a great man  CP becomes the engine

---

### 规则二：Heroine's Three Tests检验 (Rule 2: Heroine's Three Tests)

1. **Worthiness (Worthiness)**  She is inherently worthy, not only worthy after counter-attack
2. **Immediate Vengeance (Immediate Vengeance)**  Retaliate on the spot, no overnight brooding, no mental drain
3. **Agency (Agency)**  She makes choices, she is not chosen

---

### 规则三：Title's 5 Iron Laws (Rule 3: Title's 5 Iron Laws)

1. **Emotional promise**  From the title, viewers know exactly what emotional payoff to expect
2. **Contrast**  At least one word breaks expectation
3. **Verb**  Gives dynamism to the title
4. **4-character OR long-form**  Avoid 68 character middle ground
5. **Iterate high-frequency words**  "CEO / Wife / Rebirth" are fatigued; use fresh imagery

---

### 规则四：Concept's 3 Iron Laws (Rule 4: Concept's 3 Iron Laws)

1. **One sentence = emotional rollercoaster** (at least two emotional turns)
2. **Core is "what she did" not "who she ends up with"**
3. **Audience finishes feeling excited, not heavy**

---

### 规则五：CP — 他被她吸引因为她太强 (Rule 5: CP — He's Drawn Because She's Too Strong)

Appeal comes from "almost said it" and "he saw how strong she was before anyone else."

- ✅ He says "I'm not bidding, I'll wait for you to choose"  correct
-  Fate arranges their meeting  wrong

---

### 规则六：business arc是燃料 (Rule 6: Money Arc is Fuel)

The business arc drives emotional arcs and showcases her abilities, but must not overshadow the main "watch what she does" storyline.

---

### 规则七：Common Pitfalls（必须避免）(Rule 7: Common Pitfalls — Must Avoid)

1. **Avoid literal workplace drama**  equity, articles of incorporation, corporate governance; viewers watch short dramas to relax
2. **Avoid film/arthouse sensibility**  concept must have clear satisfying payoff source and conflict engine per episode
3. **Avoid social-media vlog feel**  has topicality but no plot, lacks satisfying payoff, surprise, or trope-subversion
4. **Avoid heavy tone**  "she's tragic but made it through" isn't a short drama, it's a bitter melodrama
5. **Avoid defining identity solely by "predicament"**  being laid off, being abandoned is a starting point, not an identity

## Workflow (Workflow)

### 第一步：Pre-Filter — "Do I Want to Be Her？" (Step 1: Pre-Filter — "Do I Want to Be Her?")

Core question: Within the first second, will a 2535 year old female viewer click on her?

**Heroine traits that make viewers want to click:**
- She has a "secret"  looks ordinary but hides an astonishing other side
- She holds a "trump card"  others don't know how strong she is
- She just gained some kind of "superpower"  rebirth / transmigration / mind-reading / rejuvenation
- She acts first  does what everyone wants to do but dares not
- She is the king/queen in a certain circle

**Key: Define her by what she can do, not by what others do to her.**

---

### 第二步：Comedy Formula Validation (Step 2: Comedy Formula Validation)

Once identity is determined, check whether the concept includes at least 3/5 elements. Supplement if fewer than 3.

---

### 第三步：Generate Concepts (Step 3: Generate Concepts)

Generate 3 differentiated concepts according to the output format, then run all 10 quality checks on each.

## Final Checklist（10 项） (Final Checklist (10 Items))

1. Heroine identity filtered through "Do I want to be her?"
2. Emotion is excitement, not heaviness?
3. Core is "what she does" not "who she ends up with"?
4. Title passes the 5 iron laws?
5. One-line logline is an emotional rollercoaster?
6. Heroine passes the three tests?
7. Three concepts have sufficient differentiation?
8. Three concepts cover different emotional types?
9. Passes comedy formula validation (at least 3/5 elements)?
10. Each episode has clear structure?
