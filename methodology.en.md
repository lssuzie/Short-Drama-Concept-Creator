# Short Drama Creative Methodology — 短剧创意方法论

> A structured creative methodology distilled from observations of hit short dramas in the H2 2025–Q1 2026 market.

---

## Design Philosophy (设计理念)

This methodology is not an instruction set for "having AI write scripts." Rather, it is a **creative stimulation methodology**.

Good ideas do not emerge from thin air, nor are they simple copies of market hits. Good ideas arise at the intersection of **market patterns** and **the creator's personal traits**.

**Core Insight:** Existing Agent harness tools excel at deterministic tasks like code execution, but perform poorly on creative work requiring flexibility. Direct generation with large models is flexible but fails to capture accumulated experience and methodology. This methodology positions itself between the two — using a structured approach to capture creator experience and judgment criteria while preserving the creative flexibility of large language models.

## Data Background (2025H2–Q1 2026) | 数据背景（2025H2-2026Q1）

**Audience Profile:**
- Female viewers: 67%, with 25–34 age group nearly 50%
- Youth users aged 25–44: 57%

**Four Hit Patterns:**

| Pattern | Core Formula | Market Performance |
|---|---|---|
| Rich-family sweet romance + regional contrast | Heroine uses distinct regional personality to "reshape" rich-family rules | Top works broke 10B views within 3 days of launch |
| Heroine counter-attack + ancient political intrigue | Modern mindset crushes ancient aristocrats, heroine advances step-by-step | Top works premiered with heat index exceeding 100M |
| Family comedy + identity reversal | Extreme identity misplacement creates absurd comedy | Series hit, first-day heat >50M per episode |
| Female growth + transmigration subvert-tropes | Female self-awakening / multi-heroine POV / breaking the fourth wall | Top works Spring Festival premiere exceeded 100M |

**Title Data:**
- 4-character titles most common (1,056 works) with highest total heat value (3.897B)
- High-frequency words shifted from "CEO / Wife / Rebirth" to "identity + action"

## Core Rules (核心规则)

### Rule 0: Start from Fantasy, Not from Problems (规则零：从幻想开始，不从问题开始)

Short dramas are adult fairy tales. The audience isn't here to watch their own suffering — they're here to see their suffering resolved.

Don't start with "what pain points do viewers have?" Start with "what kind of person do viewers want to become?"

**Three Sources of Fantasy:**

1. **Ability Fantasy** — She's incredible, dominating everyone in a specific domain
   - Example: crushing ancient aristocrats with modern business thinking
   - Example: using regional personality to "reshape" rich-family rules

2. **Identity Fantasy** — She has secrets — looks ordinary but hides an astonishing other side
   - Example: 18-year-old appearance with an elder's soul
   - Example: pretending to be family but becoming real family

3. **Action Fantasy** — She acts first — does what everyone wants to do but dares not
   - Example: marrying first then retaliating on the spot
   - Example: best friends transmigrating together, breaking the fourth wall

**Tone Check:**
- ✅ "Wow she's amazing, I want to be like that" → Correct
- ❌ "Sigh she's so tragic, but good thing she made it through" → Wrong, change it
- ❌ "She's so strong, I should work hard too" → Wrong, this is chicken soup, not a short drama

---

### Comedy Formula (喜剧公式)

Data from H2 2025–Q1 2026 hits shows short dramas with comedic elements break out more easily.

Comedy naturally carries: surprise, contrast, relaxation, satisfaction, status gap, situational displacement.

**Formula: Fantasy Trigger × Extreme Identity Misplacement × Family/Emotional Relations × Comedy × Healing**

| Element | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Fantasy Trigger | An absurd "trigger" that fires the story | transmigration, rejuvenation, mind-reading, rebirth |
| Extreme Identity Misplacement | Greater identity gap created by trigger is better | 18yo appearance × elder soul, best friends dual-transmigration |
| Family/Emotional Relations | Real emotional pain points beneath absurd shell | mother-daughter, mother-in-law/daughter-in-law, husband-wife, best-friend |
| Comedy | Humor from identity clash must appear every episode | daily collisions from identity contrast |
| Healing | After laughter, audience feels warmth | emotional reconciliation beneath absurd shell |

**Validation:** Describe your concept in one sentence and check if it includes at least 3 of the 5 elements.

### Strong Heroine Principle (大女主原则)

- The audience follows the **heroine**, not the CP
- Every turning point is her decision
- He falls for her because she's too strong, not because of fate
- CP is seasoning, not the main course

---

### Rule 1: "Watch What She Does" as Main Engine (规则一：主引擎是"看她怎么做")

What viewers chase isn't "will they get together?" — it's "what will she do next?"

- ✅ She crushes ancient aristocrats with modern business thinking → "what she does" is the engine
- ❌ She meets a great man → CP becomes the engine

---

### Rule 2: Heroine's Three Tests (规则二：女主三关检验)

1. **Worthiness** — She is inherently worthy, not only worthy after counter-attack
2. **Immediate Vengeance** — Retaliate on the spot, no overnight brooding, no mental drain
3. **Agency** — She makes choices, she is not chosen

---

### Rule 3: Title's 5 Iron Laws (规则三：标题 5 铁律)

1. **Emotional promise** — viewers know the emotional payoff from the title
2. **Contrast** — at least one word breaks expectation
3. **Verb** — gives dynamism
4. **4-character OR long-form** — avoid 6–8 character middle ground
5. **Iterate high-frequency words** — "CEO/Wife/Rebirth" are fatigued, use fresh imagery

---

### Rule 4: Concept's 3 Iron Laws (规则四：概念 3 铁律)

1. **One sentence = emotional rollercoaster** (at least two emotional turns)
2. **Core is "what she did" not "who she ends up with"**
3. **Audience must finish excited, not heavy**

---

### Rule 5: CP — He's Drawn Because She's Too Strong (规则五：CP — 他被她吸引因为她太强)

Appeal comes from "almost said it" and "he saw how strong she was before anyone else."

- ✅ He says "I'm not bidding, I'll wait for you to choose"
- ❌ Fate arranges their meeting

---

### Rule 6: Business Arc is Fuel (规则六：搞钱线是燃料)

The business arc drives emotional arcs and showcases her abilities, but must not overshadow the main "watch what she does" storyline.

---

### Rule 7: Common Pitfalls — Must Avoid (规则七：常见错误（必须避免）)

1. **Avoid literal workplace drama** — equity, articles of incorporation, corporate governance; viewers watch short dramas to relax
2. **Avoid film/arthouse sensibility** — concept must have clear satisfying payoff source and conflict engine per episode
3. **Avoid social-media vlog feel** — has topicality but no plot, lacks satisfying payoff, surprise, or trope-subversion
4. **Avoid heavy tone** — "she's tragic but made it through" isn't a short drama, it's a bitter melodrama
5. **Avoid defining identity solely by "predicament"** — being laid off, being abandoned is a starting point, not an identity

## Workflow (生成流程)

### Step 1: Pre-Filter — "Do I Want to Be Her?" (第一步：前置筛选 — "我想成为她吗？")

**Core question:** Within the first second, will a 25–35 year old female viewer click on her?

**Heroine traits that make viewers want to click:**
- She has a "secret" — looks ordinary but hides an astonishing other side
- She holds a "trump card" — others don't know how strong she is
- She just gained some kind of "superpower" — rebirth / transmigration / mind-reading / rejuvenation
- She acts first — does what everyone wants to do but dares not
- She is the king/queen in a certain circle

**Key:** Define her by what she can do, not by what others do to her.

---

### Step 2: Comedy Formula Validation (第二步：喜剧公式验证)

Once identity is determined, check whether the concept includes at least 3/5 elements. Supplement if fewer than 3.

---

### Step 3: Generate Concepts (第三步：生成概念)

Generate 3 differentiated concepts according to the output format, then run all 10 quality checks on each.

## Final Checklist (10 Items) | 最后检查（10 项）

1. Does the heroine's identity pass the "Do I want to be her?" screening?
2. Is the emotion excitement rather than heaviness?
3. Is the core "what she did" not "who she ends up with"?
4. Does the title pass the 5 iron laws?
5. Is the one-line logline an emotional rollercoaster?
6. Does the heroine pass the three tests?
7. Do the three concepts have sufficient differentiation?
8. Do the three concepts cover different emotional types?
9. Does it pass the comedy formula validation (at least 3/5 elements)?
10. Does each episode have clear structure?
